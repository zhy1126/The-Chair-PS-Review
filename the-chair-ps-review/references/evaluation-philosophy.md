# Evaluation Philosophy

## The purpose of a PS

Treat a PS as an argument about fit, not a showcase of general excellence.

The essay must explain why this applicant's trajectory, questions, abilities, and needs match this particular programme. Experiences and achievements matter only as evidence within that argument.

Distinguish three claims:

- **Why me:** what the applicant can already bring.
- **Why this programme:** what the programme specifically provides.
- **Why this fit:** why the applicant's prior trajectory, present need, programme resources, and future use form one credible relationship.

Prioritise the third claim.

## Match over merit display

Do not reward GPA, rank, scholarships, awards, prestigious organisations, or famous competitions merely for being impressive. These facts usually belong in the CV, transcript, or application form.

Flag GPA in a PS when it functions as self-praise or duplicates the transcript. Allow an exception only when the prompt requests it, a material academic anomaly needs explanation, or the number is indispensable to a genuine development narrative.

Prefer a modest but highly relevant experience over a prestigious but disconnected one.

## Depth over breadth

Treat word count as an opportunity cost. Every additional experience reduces the space available to explain another experience's meaning.

Prefer a small number of core experiences that reveal:

1. A concrete problem, tension, or observation.
2. The applicant's reasoning, method, or decision.
3. Evidence or a result.
4. A reflection, limitation, or change of mind.
5. A next action or training need.

Do not infer depth from long descriptions, technical nouns, action verbs, or institutional prestige.

## Coherence over inventory

Require the draft to have one central line. Paragraphs must alter, deepen, test, or extend what came before.

Chronology is not logic. A sequence of undergraduate study, competition, internship, exchange, and career plan remains a CV unless the reader can see why each step changed the applicant's direction or understanding.

## Thought over polish

Treat fluent English as execution, not substance. Identify generic or AI-smoothed prose when it contains elegant transitions but no applicant-specific judgement.

Critical thinking must be observable. Look for competing interpretations, methods, evidence, uncertainty, limitations, and revised conclusions.

## Specificity over promotion

Require programme resources to serve a stated need. A course, professor, centre, clinic, location, or network matters only when the applicant explains:

- what unresolved question or capability gap it addresses;
- why that resource is unusually suitable;
- how the applicant will use the resulting training.

Reject praise that could be pasted into another university's essay.

## Truth over engineered narrative

Do not reward a story for appearing perfectly linear. Real intellectual development may include uncertainty, failed assumptions, or incomplete answers.

Flag causal links that are unsupported by chronology or the applicant's actual role. Never recommend inventing a turning point, expanding responsibility, or claiming an insight the applicant cannot defend.

## Diagnosis over ghostwriting

Keep authorship with the applicant. Provide questions, structural alternatives, priority decisions, and schematic placeholders. Do not supply a complete replacement paragraph or essay.

The goal is to help the applicant discover and articulate their own match, not to manufacture one for them.
