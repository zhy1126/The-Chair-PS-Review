# Qualitative Diagnostic Framework

## Hard risks

Check these before making an overall judgement:

- Wrong institution, programme, degree, or prompt.
- Material word-limit or format violation.
- Claims that appear invented, chronologically impossible, or unsupported by the stated role.
- No recoverable applicant-programme fit thesis when the prompt requires one.
- The essay is dominated by CV repetition.

A hard risk overrides fluent language and local strengths.

## Diagnostic dimensions

For each dimension, classify the draft as `convincing`, `developing`, `structural risk`, or `not assessable`. Support the classification with textual evidence and explain its consequence for revision.

| Dimension | Convincing | Developing | Structural risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Positioning and match | Applicant trajectory, specific need, programme resources, and future use form a precise relationship | A relevant direction exists, but one or more links remain generic or incomplete | The essay proves general excellence, praises the university, or lacks a recoverable fit relationship |
| Main line and progression | Every core paragraph deepens, tests, or advances one coherent trajectory | A central theme is visible, but some paragraphs are detachable or transitions are asserted | Experiences are listed or juxtaposed; chronology substitutes for logic |
| Depth of thought | The essay shows questions, reasoning or method, evidence, limits, changed judgement, and further inquiry | Reflection is present but uneven or stops at lessons learned | Tasks, technical nouns, and outcomes replace thought |
| Experience selection and evidence | A small, sufficient set of experiences provides concrete, non-duplicative evidence | Most experiences are relevant but some are compressed, repetitive, or weakly connected | Prestige signals or excessive experience inventory prevent depth and coherence |
| Task compliance and professionalism | The draft answers the actual prompt within its constraints in concise, credible language | Requirements are mostly met but local generic phrasing, compression, or evidence issues remain | Prompt, programme, format, factual credibility, or transcript-repetition problems undermine the response |

Use `not assessable` when the relevant prompt, programme information, CV, or factual context was not supplied. Never convert missing context into a negative judgement about the draft.

## Overall verdicts

Choose the closest description and explain the decisive evidence:

- **Convincing fit argument:** preserve the structure and make local refinements.
- **Promising but incomplete trajectory:** preserve the intellectual or professional core and complete missing links.
- **CV-like merit inventory:** reduce and reselect experiences before sentence editing.
- **Generic motivation essay:** replace transferable praise and aspiration with applicant-specific reasoning.
- **Structurally mispositioned response:** return to the prompt, positioning, and material selection.

Do not collapse the verdict into a total score. Different prompts legitimately change what a strong response must contain.

## Issue severity

- **Structural:** affects positioning, central line, experience selection, or the fit thesis. Requires re-outline or re-selection.
- **Major:** affects one paragraph's reasoning, evidence, credibility, or connection. Requires paragraph reconstruction.
- **Local:** affects wording, grammar, compression, or a single transition. Requires sentence-level editing.

## CV-likeness indicators

Flag several of these together as a structural pattern:

- GPA, rank, scholarships, or awards used as proof of worth.
- Repeated organisation, role, competition, and result naming.
- More experiences than the word limit can develop.
- Sentences dominated by `participated`, `worked`, `won`, `assisted`, or `was responsible for`.
- Each paragraph can be reordered without changing the argument.
- Reflection is limited to generic skills gained.
- Programme courses are attached to experiences after the fact without a demonstrated need.

## Match test

For every programme-specific claim, require all three links:

1. **Backward link:** the prior question, experience, or limitation that creates the need.
2. **Resource link:** the specific programme element that addresses it.
3. **Forward link:** the research, practice, or career use enabled by that training.

Missing links indicate incomplete or superficial match. When official programme information was not supplied or verified, mark the resource link `not assessable` rather than guessing.
